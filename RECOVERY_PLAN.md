# Recovery Plan — getting back on track

You missed a couple of days for personal reasons. That's normal and completely recoverable.
Good news first: **nothing is actually lost.** Days 4 and 5 are already written and waiting
(problems + playgrounds), and you're only mid-way through Day 3. The "damage" was just the
calendar drifting — so we re-baselined the tracker to start from today. No red, no guilt.

This plan does two things: (1) a calm catch-up schedule that does **not** make you cram, and
(2) guardrails so a future missed day can never snowball again.

---

## The mindset (read this first)
- **A missed day is a pause, not a failure.** The goal is mastery, not a perfect streak.
- **Never "make up" by doing two full days back-to-back.** That overload is exactly what causes
  the next burnout-skip. We re-baseline forward instead of cramming backward.
- **Something > nothing, always.** A 30-minute rescue session beats a zero and keeps the chain alive.

## The 5 guardrails (so this can't snowball again)

**1. Minimum Viable Day (MVD).**
On a bad day, you are allowed to do *just* the ✅ core questions and skip the ⭐ stretch. Finishing
the core of a day counts as "done." Never skip a whole day when a half-day is possible.

**2. The 50% rule.**
No time for a full 2-hour session? Do *one hour*. Half a session keeps momentum and stops the
mental "I'm behind" spiral. Half-days are logged as progress, not failures.

**3. One weekly buffer day — always.**
Every week already has a Revision day (Day 7, 14, 21, 28). Treat those as free catch-up slots.
Never schedule yourself 7-for-7; the buffer absorbs the inevitable slip.

**4. One free "skip token" per week.**
You get to miss one day a week, guilt-free, no make-up required — just re-baseline forward. Using
your token is *part of the plan*, not breaking it. (Two skips in a week = signal to lighten the
daily load, not to push harder.)

**5. Re-baseline, don't backlog.**
When you fall behind, we move the dates — we do NOT stack missed days onto future ones. The tracker
should always show a realistic schedule starting from *today*, so you never open it to a wall of red.

---

## If you fall behind again — the 3-step reset
1. **Re-baseline the dates** (I can do this in one command — just say "re-baseline my tracker").
2. **Restart with the lightest unfinished day** to rebuild momentum (a quick win first).
3. **Add a buffer day** within 3 days of restarting.

That's the whole recovery loop. It's designed so the *worst case* is a few days of date-shifting,
never a collapse of the whole plan.
